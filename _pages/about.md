I'm a creative technologist and Operations Manager based in Central Illinois.

Whether I'm building a mobile app or a metadata pipeline, I've always asked the same thing: What is this actually supposed to do for the person using it? I've just asked it at very different scales.

I started as a public affairs specialist for the United States Air Force, telling stories for the Air Force and Department of Defense. That training stuck with me: find the human detail, don't waste the audience's time, and never compromise on accuracy or integrity when communicating with the public and the media.

Later, in broadcast tech, I designed and implemented automation systems with AudioVAULT. I collaborated with stations to achieve specific goals, and sometimes completely modernized the entire operation from the ground up to run local and satellite programming while feeding metadata to RDS, HD radio, and live streaming pipelines that had to run 24/7, reliably and consistently. I still have footprints, and a lot of friends, in stations across the country from that work.

At SIU School of Medicine, I led the redesign of what became siumed.org. We took a massive, confusing legacy site and rebuilt it on Drupal with a clear goal: help people find a doctor without the headache. That same Drupal instance also supports the teaching side of the school.

Now I'm Operations Manager at Commotion, a small team building white-label mobile apps for radio stations with real-time engagement and monetization baked in. My role spans customer integration, support, design, and testing. Working inside a live app platform every day is what pushed me from web-based solutions toward mobile ones.

Outside the day job, I run Blackdaw Media LLC. I build indie tools for creative people who want less friction between having an idea and recording it. My flagship app, Intent Photo Notes, was built because I wanted to capture why I took a photo, not just the technical settings.
Photography is how I pay attention. I shoot infrared, street, and wildlife, anything that makes me stop. It's the same instinct I use at work: looking at a thing until I understand what it's actually for.

This site is where the work lives. The journal is where I think out loud.
