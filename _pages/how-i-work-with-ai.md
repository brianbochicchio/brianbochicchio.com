AI is a force multiplier in my work — not a replacement for craft, judgment, or accountability.

I use AI tools deliberately and collaboratively. The thinking, the decisions, the creative direction, and the final call are always mine. I stand behind everything I ship, regardless of what tools helped me build it.

**My working principles:**

**Privacy and local-first.** The same values baked into my apps apply to my workflow. I minimize third-party data exposure and don't feed sensitive client or user information into AI systems.

**Trust but verify.** AI output gets read, questioned, and tested — not blindly shipped. Pattern matching isn't the same as understanding, and I know the difference.

**Honest about the process.** I’m transparent about my process, but I don't lead with the tool—because the tool isn't the story. The work is.

**Co-creation, not automation.** I don't use AI to skip the hard parts. I use it to think better, move faster, and punch above my weight as a solo developer and creative technologist.

This is how I work. It'll evolve as the tools do — but the principles won't.
